Ministère des Armées

Ground forces

The French Army, also known as the "Land Army", is the main force and power of France. It is also the most important part of the Armed Forces of France. This part of the army is led by the CEMAT (who is the Chief of Staff). He is also the subordinate of the CEMA (Chief of the Defense Staff). This CEMA commands the units which are active in the Army and himself or herself answers to the President. Furthermore, the CEMAT is responsible to the Ministry of the Armed Forces concerning operations of preparation, administration and equipment. This part of the French forces, since the revolution, has been mostly composed of professionals and conscripts. But since the Parliament stopped conscription, it is now considered as a professional force.

Navy

"La Marine Nationale" is the correct name for the French Navy. Previously called "La Royale" during its creation, it is the maritime branch of the French Armed Forces. It is considered as one of the most powerful naval forces in the world. It ranks seventh when comparing tonnage and is fifth in number of vessels. This naval force is one of eight operating fixed wing aircraft carriers. Its flagship is called "Charles de Gaulle" and is the only nuclear powered carrier in the world outside of the ones in the USA's navy. Since the French Navy is a blue-water one, it is capable of operating worldwide and is able to conduct any kind of expeditionary missions whoile having a large averseas presence. As of 2021, it employed 44,000 personnel, more than 180 vessels and 200 aircrafts.

Air force

Air and Space Force is considered to be part of the same branch in the French Armed Forces, aslo called the AAE (Armée de l'air et de l'Espace). It was formed in 1909 as an arm of the French Army. However, it has now become completely independant since 1934 and assume the name Air and Space Force in 2020. During this year, as an "evolution to its missions", it was determined that it was this branch that was responsible for any operations related to outer space.40,500 personnel was recorded to be employed in the Air and Space Force. Its command is called the CEMAAE (Chief of Staff of the French Air and Space Force). He or she is a direct subordinate to the CEMA. The number of operational aircrafts is not confirmed. The force is composed of mainly Dassault Mirage and Dassault Rafale.

Gendarmerie

The National Gendarmerie is one of two national law enforcement forces of France, along with the National Police. The Gendarmerie is a branch of the French Armed Forces placed under the jurisdiction of the Ministry of the Interior, with additional duties from the Ministry of Armed Forces. Its responsibilities include policing smaller towns, suburbs and rural areas, along with special subdivisions like the GSPR. On the other hand, the National Police is a civilian law enforcement agency that is in charge of policing cities and larger towns. Because of its military status, the Gendarmerie also performs a range of military and defence missions, including having a cybercrime division. The Gendarmerie has a strength of around 102,269 people as of 2022.


ORGANISATION

The Army comprises:

  • The Army Chief of Staff (Chef d'état-major de l'armée de Terre (CEMAT)).
  • The army staff (l'état-major de l'armée de Terre or EMAT), which gives general direction and management of all the components;
  • The Army Inspectorate (l'inspection de l'armée de Terre);
  • The Army Human Resources Directorate (la direction des ressources humaines de l'armée de Terre or DRHAT);
  • The forces;
  • A territorial organisation
  • The services;
  • The personnel training and military higher training organisms.

    • The French Army was reorganized in 2016. The new organisation consists of two combined divisions (carrying the heritage of 1st Armored and 3rd Armored divisions) and given three combat brigades to supervise each. There is also the Franco-German Brigade. The 4th Airmobile Brigade was reformed to direct the three combat helicopter regiments. There are also several division-level (niveau divisionnaire) specialized commands including Intelligence, Information and communication systems, Maintenance, Logistics, Special Forces, Army Light Aviation, Foreign Legion, National Territory, Training.

    CONTRACT

    Contracts for soldiers and personnel in the French Army range from one to five years. However, they are all renewable. Non commissioned officers serve on contracts that are permanent or five years in very specific cases. A high school diploma is however necessary to apply to a NCO post. École Nationale des Sous-Officiers d’Active (ENSOA), Basic NCO school of 8 months, followed by combat school of 4 to 36 weeks depending on occupational specialty. A small number of NCO candidates are trained at the Ecole Militaire de Haute Montagne (EMHM) (High Mountain Military School). NCOs with the Advanced Army Technician Certificate (BSTAT) can serve as platoon leaders.

    On the other hand, Contract officers serve on renewable contracts for a maximum of 20 years service and a bachelor's degree is necessary. There exists two programs; Combat Officers & Specialist Officers. In both programs, the graduates will start as Second Lieutenants and could reach Lieutenant Colonels rank afterward. Combat officers spend eight months at ESM, followed by one year at a combat school. Specialist officers spend three months at ESM, followed by a year of on the job-training within an area of specialization determined by the type of degree held.
